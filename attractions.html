Landmarks and neighborhoods

Top Places to Visit

These attractions show different sides of Shanghai: colonial-era architecture, traditional gardens, futuristic towers, and walkable neighborhoods.

The Bund

The Bund is the classic starting point for a Shanghai trip. It offers views of both historic buildings and the modern Pudong skyline.

  • Best time: evening or sunrise
  • Good for: skyline photos
  • Nearby: Nanjing Road

Yu Garden

Yu Garden gives visitors a more traditional view of the city through pavilions, ponds, rockeries, and old-style shopping streets.

  • Best time: morning
  • Good for: traditional architecture
  • Nearby: City God Temple area

Lujiazui

Lujiazui is the financial district of Shanghai. It is useful for understanding the city’s modern image and rapid development.

  • Best time: late afternoon
  • Good for: skyscrapers
  • Nearby: Oriental Pearl Tower

Former French Concession

This area is known for tree-lined streets, cafes, boutiques, and older residential architecture. It is ideal for a slow walk.

  • Best time: afternoon
  • Good for: walking and cafes
  • Nearby: Tianzifang and Xintiandi

Shanghai Disney Resort

For families or theme park fans, Shanghai Disney can be a full-day experience. It should be planned separately from downtown sightseeing.

  • Best time: weekday
  • Good for: families
  • Nearby: Disneytown

Suggested Order

  1. Start with Yu Garden in the morning.
  2. Walk or ride to Nanjing Road.
  3. Visit the Bund near sunset.
  4. Cross to Lujiazui for night views.

This order reduces backtracking and gives a natural contrast between old Shanghai and modern Shanghai.

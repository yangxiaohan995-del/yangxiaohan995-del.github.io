Shanghai Food Guide

Shanghai food is often associated with a slightly sweet and rich flavor. A good food route should mix street snacks, noodles, and classic restaurant dishes.

What should you try first?

First-time visitors often start with dumplings and buns because they are easy to find and strongly connected with the city’s food identity. After that, a sit-down Shanghainese meal is useful for tasting richer dishes.

Recommended Foods

Use this list as a simple starting point.

Xiaolongbao

Soup dumplings with thin wrappers and hot broth inside. Eat carefully so the soup does not spill.

Shengjianbao

Pan-fried buns with a crispy bottom and juicy filling. They are usually served with sesame and scallions.

Scallion Oil Noodles

A simple noodle dish flavored with scallion oil and soy sauce. It is affordable, quick, and very local.
